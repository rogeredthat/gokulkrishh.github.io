The Albums

the challenge

The Albums founders wanted to launch a responsive minimum viable product fast that worked right away on major platforms - including mobile, tablets and web.

the Launchyard solution

We settled on Zurb Foundation which is a responsive front-end framework that allowed the application to work seamlessly across different platforms. We focused on tight release cycles with smaller feature sets, allowing them to launch quickly and iterate.

Picquity

the challenge

Picquity is an image centric web application, and the challenge was to manage massive image processing intensive operations (for instance: grabbing images from different websites, fingerprinting them and matching against a massive list of online images), store and access it on demand.

the Launchyard solution

We went to work leveraging a non-sql database (Mongodb) to solve the unstructured data problem and identified robust solutions that we could implement for queuing and processing the images asynchronously. Our solution allowed them to launch their product with the sophisticated functionality they required relatively quickly.
